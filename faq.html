Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

General Questions

Q: Do I need to submit a new form to change my data?
A: You can either submit a new form ensuring to select "I'm already listed, but need to update my information" or just text the Wizard at 603-921-0994.

Q: What is the Free Snake Relocation Directory?
A: The Free Snake Relocation Directory is a volunteer-based network that connects individuals with volunteer snake relocators who will assist with removing and relocating snakes free of charge.

Q: How do I find a snake relocator near me?
A: Use our interactive map to find a volunteer relocator in your area. If no one is available nearby, check back later or consider local wildlife control options.

Q: Will I be charged?
A: No. All volunteers listed in the Free Snake Relocation Directory have agreed to provide snake relocation services at no cost. Some are licensed professionals who may offer separate paid services—but they are not permitted to charge for relocations through this directory or our Facebook group. Make sure you mention that you found the relocation via the Free Snake Relocation Directory upon initial contact.

Q: What areas does the directory cover?
A: We have relocators across various states. Check our interactive map to see availability in your area.

Snake Safety & Identification

Q: What should I do if I see a snake?
A: Stay calm! Keep a safe distance, avoid sudden movements, and do not attempt to handle the snake yourself. Call a relocator for assistance.

Q: Are all snakes dangerous?
A: No! Most snakes are harmless and play an important role in controlling pests like rodents and insects.

Q: Can I kill a snake if I feel threatened?
A: Many states have laws protecting native snakes. Killing a snake is often unnecessary and could be illegal. Contact a relocator for help instead.

Requesting a Snake Relocation

Q: How quickly can a relocator arrive?
A: Response time varies depending on availability and location. Some relocators may be able to respond immediately, while others may take longer.

Q: What information should I provide when requesting a relocation?
A: Try to provide:

  • Your location (city, state, and nearest landmark)
  • A description of the snake (size, color, pattern)
  • The snake’s current location (inside the house, yard, etc.)
  • A photo if possible (but only if it’s safe to take one!)

Q: Will the relocator remove the snake from inside my home?
A: Yes, most relocators will safely remove snakes from homes, garages, and other enclosed areas.

Q: What happens to the snake after it is relocated?
A: Relocators release snakes into suitable habitats, ensuring they can continue their natural role in the ecosystem.

Becoming a Volunteer Relocator

Q: How do I sign up to be a relocator?
A: Visit our Relocator Registration Form to apply. Volunteers should have experience handling snakes and knowledge of local species. Please read the disclaimer before registering.

Q: Do I need a permit to relocate snakes?
A: Some states require permits for wildlife handling. Check your local regulations before signing up.

Q: Can I get training to become a relocator?
A: We recommend reaching out to local herpetological societies, wildlife organizations, or experienced relocators for hands-on training.
